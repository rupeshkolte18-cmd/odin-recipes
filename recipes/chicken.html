Chicken curry

To make a classic Indian chicken curry, start by heating cooking fat (oil or ghee) in a large pan over medium heat. Fry whole spices like bay leaf, cinnamon, cardamom, and cloves for a minute until they release their aroma. Add finely chopped onions and sauté them until they turn a deep golden brown, then stir in ginger-garlic paste and green chillies for another minute. Next, add the ground spices—including turmeric, coriander, cumin, and chilli powder—along with pureed tomatoes, cooking the mixture until the oil begins to separate from the sauce. Toss in the chicken pieces, coating them thoroughly in the spice base, then pour in water or broth and bring it to a boil. Reduce the heat, cover the pan, and let everything simmer for about 25 to 30 minutes until the chicken is tender and cooked through. Finally, stir in garam masala, finish with a squeeze of fresh lemon juice, and garnish with chopped coriander leaves before serving hot.

Ingredients

The Foundation

  • Chicken: 1 kg of bone-in pieces (cut into medium chunks) provides the richest flavour, though boneless chicken thighs or breasts can be substituted.
  • Cooking Fat: 3–4 tablespoons of vegetable oil, ghee (clarified butter), or mustard oil.
  • Aromatics: 2–3 large onions (finely chopped), 1–2 tablespoons of ginger-garlic paste, and 2–4 green chillies.
  • Liquid Base: 2–3 chopped tomatoes (or 1 cup tomato puree) and 1–2 cups of water or chicken broth.

Core Spice Blend

  • Turmeric Powder: 1 teaspoon (for earthy flavor and golden color).
  • Coriander Powder: 2 teaspoons.
  • Cumin Powder: 1 teaspoon.
  • Chili Powder or Paprika: 1–2 teaspoons (adjusted for heat tolerance).
  • Garam Masala: 1 teaspoon (stirred in at the end of cooking).
  • Salt: To taste

Steps

1. Sauté Aromatics

  1. Heat fat: Add 3 tablespoons oil or ghee to a heavy-bottomed pot over medium heat
  2. Bloom whole spices: Fry bay leaf, cinnamon, cardamom, and cloves for 1 minute until fragrant.
  3. Brown onions: Add finely chopped onions and cook for 10–12 minutes until deep golden brown.
  4. Add paste: Stir in ginger-garlic paste and green chillies; sauté for 1 minute until raw smell disappears.

2. Build Spice Base

  1. Incorporate ground spices: Add turmeric, coriander, cumin, chili powder, and salt.
  2. Toast briefly: Stir continuously for 30 seconds on low heat to avoid burning.
  3. Cook tomatoes: Pour in tomato puree. Cook for 5–7 minutes until oil separates at the edges

3. Simmer Chicken

  1. Sear meat: Add chicken pieces. Toss for 5 minutes on high heat until the exterior turns white.
  2. Add liquid: Pour in 1 cup of hot water or chicken broth and stir well.
  3. Simmer covered: Bring to a boil, then cover and cook on low heat for 25 minutes.

4. Finish and Garnish

  1. Check doneness: Ensure chicken is tender and cooked through to an internal temperature of 74°C.
  2. Add final aromatics: Stir in garam masala and turn off the heat.
  3. Brighten flavor: Mix in 1 tablespoon of fresh lemon juice.
  4. Serve: Garnish with freshly chopped coriander leaves.
