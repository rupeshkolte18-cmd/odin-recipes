Palak Paneer

Palak Paneer is a classic North Indian vegetarian curry made of soft cottage cheese cubes (paneer) simmered in a smooth, vibrant green spinach (palak) sauce. This nutritious, iron-rich dish is perfectly seasoned with garlic, ginger, and aromatic spices. It pairs beautifully with garlic naan, roti, or jeera rice.

Ingredients

  • Spinach: 1 large bunch (around 250–300g)
  • Paneer: 200g, cut into cubes
  • Aromatics: 1 medium onion (finely chopped), 1 tbsp chopped garlic, 1 tsp ginger paste
  • Spices: 1 tsp cumin seeds, ½ tsp turmeric powder, 1 tsp red chilli powder, ½ tsp garam masala
  • Fat & Cream: 2 tbsp ghee or oil, 2 tbsp fresh cream (optional, for richness)

Steps

  1. Blanch the Spinach: Drop cleaned spinach leaves into a pot of rolling boiled water for 1–2 minutes. Immediately transfer them into ice-cold water to stop cooking and retain the vibrant green colour. Drain well and blend into a smooth puree.
  2. Build the Curry Base: Heat ghee or oil in a pan. Add cumin seeds and let them splutter. Sauté the garlic, ginger, and chopped onions until translucent.
  3. Simmer and Finish: Add your turmeric, chilli powder, and the spinach puree to the pan. Cook for 2 minutes. Gently fold in the paneer cubes, garam masala, and fresh cream. Simmer on low heat for 3 minutes so the paneer absorbs the flavours. Serve hot
