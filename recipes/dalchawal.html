Dal-Chawal

Dal Chawal is the quintessential Indian comfort food consisting of simmered, spiced lentils (dal) served over fluffy steamed rice (chawal). It is an absolute staple across South Asian households, prized for being nutritious, inexpensive, and incredibly soothing. When lentils and rice are eaten together, they create a complete plant-based protein source containing all nine essential amino acids.

Ingredients

  • Lentils: Most commonly made with Toor dal (arhar/pigeon peas), Moong dal (yellow lentils), or Masoor dal (red lentils).
  • Rice: Standard long-grain Basmati rice or regular white/brown daily-use rice.
  • Aromatics: Onions, tomatoes, ginger, garlic, and green chillies.
  • Tadka (Tempering): Ghee or oil infused with cumin seeds, hing (asafoetida), mustard seeds, and dry red chillies.

Steps

  1. Boil the Dal: Rinse 1 cup of lentils and pressure cook with 3 cups of water, a pinch of turmeric, and salt for 3–4 whistles until soft and
  2. Cook the Rice: Wash 1 cup of rice and boil or steam it until fluffy.
  3. Make the Tadka: Heat ghee or oil in a pan. Splutter cumin seeds and hing. Sauté chopped onions, ginger-garlic paste, and tomatoes until soft.
  4. Combine: Pour the sizzling tempering over the boiled dal and simmer for 2 minutes. Serve piping hot over the rice.
